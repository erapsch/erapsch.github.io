Welcome to my **academic homepage**! I am a **mathematician** with expertise in **quantitative and game-theoretic modelling, risk and uncertainty analysis, and data-driven methods**. Getting deeply to the **theoretical core of problems and ideas** and providing insights that **create value in real-world decisions** are to me two sides of the same coin.

To find out more about my **work outside academia**, see my <a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/erapsch">LinkedIn page</a>. As a Decision Scientist, I contribute to the decision intelligence platform of my employer <a href="https://tulana.de/">tulana</a>.

I obtained a **PhD in Mathematics** from Technische Universität Berlin, following studies in Berlin, at the University of Oxford, Ecole normale supérieure (ENS) de Lyon, and the University of Freiburg. I am experienced in international research environments and interdisciplinary collaboration, and worked as a full-time Research and Teaching Associate at TU Berlin for several years, lastly under <a href="https://www3.math.tu-berlin.de/stoch/wp_bank/">Professor Dr. Peter Bank</a>. Find out more <a href="/CV">here</a>.

My academic research is concerned with topics in **economic theory** and mathematical **probability theory**, and especially the link between both. I am also interested in applications based on insights derived from models using theoretical concepts. This covers especially game theory, decision theory, stochastic analysis and control, financial mathematics, and climate economics. More details can be found on the subpage on my <a href="/research">research</a>.

To get an idea of my academic research, click <a href="https://doi.org/10.14279/depositonce-24410">here</a> and take a look at my **doctoral dissertation**, including a preface for non-experts and experts alike. Click <a href="{{ site.baseurl }}/files\On_the_Foundations_of_Dynamic_Games_and_Probability__Decision_Making_in_Stochastic_Extensive_Form.pdf">here</a> to read the slides for the 30-minute talk at my doctoral defence, which provides an overview of the dissertation.

The second main pillar of my academic activity has been <a href="/teaching">**teaching**</a> **in mathematics**. I have taught mainly in courses on probability theory, financial mathematics, and analysis, mostly for pure mathematics programmes, but also for business mathematics and engineering programmes.
